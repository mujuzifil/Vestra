# Stage 18.7 ‚Äî Credit Management Report

## Objective

Provide a controlled credit facility for approved distributors, including authorization at checkout, capture against invoices, release on cancellation, payment recording, and administrator-managed limits.

## Credit Account & Transactions

Each distributor has one `CreditAccount` record with:

- `limit` ‚Äî the maximum credit extended by VESTRA.
- `balance` ‚Äî the amount currently owed.
- `authorized_amount` ‚Äî credit reserved for pending orders awaiting fulfillment.
- `status` ‚Äî `pending` or `active`.

Every movement is recorded in `credit_transactions` via the `CreditTransaction` model. Supported transaction types are:

- `authorization` ‚Äî credit reserved when an order is placed.
- `capture` ‚Äî reserved credit converted to owed balance upon fulfillment.
- `release` ‚Äî reservation reversed (e.g., order cancelled).
- `payment` ‚Äî balance reduced by a received payment.
- `limit_change` ‚Äî audit record when an admin adjusts the credit limit.

## Authorization / Capture / Release / Payment Flows

`App\Services\CreditAccountService` provides the core state transitions:

- `authorize(Distributor, amount, Order)` ‚Äî checks the account is `active` and that `amount <= availableCredit()`, then increments `authorized_amount` and writes an `authorization` transaction.
- `capture(Distributor, amount, Order)` ‚Äî decrements `authorized_amount` and increments `balance`, recording a `capture` transaction.
- `release(Distributor, amount, Order)` ‚Äî decrements `authorized_amount` and records a `release` transaction.
- `payment(Distributor, amount, reference, createdBy)` ‚Äî decrements `balance` and records a `payment` transaction.

The `CreditAccount` model computes available credit as:

```
max(0, limit - balance - authorized_amount)
```

It also reports a utilization percentage based on used credit versus the limit.

## Credit Limit Enforcement at Checkout

When a distributor selects `credit` as the payment method during checkout, `DistributorOrderService` calls `CreditAccountService::authorize()` before finalizing the order. If the account is not active or the order total exceeds the available credit, a `422 Unprocessable Entity` response is returned with an error on `payment_method`.

The automated test suite verifies both successful credit checkout and rejection when the limit is insufficient.

## Admin-Managed Limits

Administrators can set or update a distributor's credit limit through `CreditAccountService::setLimit()`. The method activates a pending account, updates the `limit`, and writes a `limit_change` transaction recording the old and new values. This gives the business team full control over exposure while preserving an audit trail.

## Frontend Visibility

The distributor dashboard displays:

- Credit limit
- Available credit
- Outstanding balance
- Credit utilization

These values are returned by `DashboardController` and rendered in the `CreditLimitCard` component.
